# Knowledge Base — Meta Ad Copy Engine

Distilled, cited expertise for writing Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad copy for DTC products. Every threshold below is sourced; see the **Sources** list at the bottom.

---

## 1. The three text fields & character limits (2025–2026)

A Meta ad has up to three text fields, each with a different job and a different truncation behavior. Write each **independently** — Meta may reposition them per person via "optimize text per person."

| Field | Job | Recommended | Hard max | Truncation reality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary Text** | The body copy above the creative | ~125 chars visible; front-load | API up to ~4,096 (UI ~2,200) | Only **~125 chars show before "See more"** on mobile Feed; **~1% of users expand** |
| **Headline** | Caption below the image | **≤40 chars** | 40 | Facebook Feed shows ~**27**; Reels overlay as low as **10** |
| **Description** | Optional reinforcement | **≤25–30 chars** | 30 | Shown reliably only on Marketplace, Search Results, Audience Network, In-stream |

Placement-specific notes: Instagram Feed 125 primary / 40 headline; Stories 125 / 40 (no description); **Reels is most restrictive** — FB Reels ~40 primary / 55 headline, Reels overlay ~60 primary / 10 headline. ([adsuploader — Meta Ad Copy Specs](https://adsuploader.com/blog/meta-ad-copy-specs), [Meta Business Help — text in ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/223409425500940))

**The single most important rule:** because ~99% of viewers never tap "See more," the **first ~125 characters (better: first ~80–100) must be a complete mini-ad** — hook + core idea. Everything after "See more" is for the 1% who lean in. ([adsuploader](https://adsuploader.com/blog/meta-ad-copy-specs))

**Multiple options per field:** Meta lets you enter up to **5 primary texts, 5 headlines, and 5 descriptions** per ad and mix-matches them per user. Always ship at least **2 primary + 2 headlines** so the system (and your read) can learn. ([adsuploader](https://adsuploader.com/blog/meta-ad-copy-specs))

## 2. Eugene Schwartz's 5 Awareness Levels → what the copy must do

From *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966); still the operating model for DTC creative strategy. The awareness level tells you **how much you must teach before you can sell**, and therefore the hook style. ([Motion — 5 customer awareness stages](https://motionapp.com/blog/five-customer-awareness-stages-advertising), [RunwayFive — 5 Stages of Awareness](https://runwayfive.com/5-stages-of-awareness/))

| Level | Buyer's state | What the copy leads with | Hook style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know they have the problem | A relatable story/observation that surfaces the problem; big idea, no product yet | Story, pattern-interrupt, "did you know" |
| **Problem-aware** | Feels the pain, doesn't know solutions exist | Empathize with the pain + hint a solution exists (a little mystery) | Problem callout (third-person-safe), curiosity gap |
| **Solution-aware** | Knows solution categories, not your product | Why *this* category/approach is the best route | Comparison, "the [category] that finally…" |
| **Product-aware** | Knows your product, not convinced | Differentiation + proof + offer | Reviews, mechanism, "why us vs. them" |
| **Most-aware** | Ready, needs a reason now | The offer, urgency, guarantee | Deal, scarcity, direct CTA |

**Practical DTC mapping:** problem-aware audiences see problem-focused ads; solution-aware see differentiation-focused ads; product-aware see conversion-focused ads. ([Motion](https://motionapp.com/blog/five-customer-awareness-stages-advertising)) For **cold prospecting**, default to **Problem-aware** — it's the workhorse for DTC. Only warm/retargeting (Product-/Most-aware) traffic tolerates direct offer/urgency copy. Nick Theriot's DTC frameworks likewise build a "5 Stages Framework" so each creative matches the viewer's psychological state rather than blasting one message. ([Nick Theriot — The Direct Response Podcast](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBVgPnCPXb4))

## 3. Copy frameworks (one per primary text — never blend)

From the anchor skill (Corey Haines' marketing skills) plus classic direct-response. ([coreyhaines31/marketingskills — ads SKILL.md](https://github.com/coreyhaines31/marketingskills/blob/main/skills/ads/SKILL.md))

- **PAS — Problem · Agitate · Solve** → best for **pain-led** Variant A. `[Problem] → [twist the knife] → [product as relief] → [CTA]`.
- **BAB — Before · After · Bridge** → best for **benefit-led** Variant B. `[current painful state] → [desired future state] → [product is the bridge]`.
- **Social-Proof Lead** → `[impressive stat/testimonial] → [what you do] → [CTA]`. Great when you have real numbers ("Loved by 40,000+…").
- **AIDA — Attention · Interest · Desire · Action** → general-purpose.
- **4Ps — Promise · Picture · Proof · Push** → offer-forward, good for Most-aware.

**Rule:** exactly one framework per primary text block; keep to **one value prop + one proof element + one CTA**. ([coreyhaines31](https://github.com/coreyhaines31/marketingskills/blob/main/skills/ads/SKILL.md))

## 4. Hook patterns (the first line does the work)

For DTC Meta, the hook is the whole game. Underlying mechanisms that make hooks work: **pattern interruption**, **curiosity gaps**, and **social validation** from frame one. ([Motion — 25 video ad hooks that convert 2025](https://motionapp.com/blog/best-dtc-meta-ad-hooks-2025))

Copy-line hook archetypes (translate the video-hook logic into text):
- **Problem callout (policy-safe):** third-person or product-first, e.g. "The reason [routine] never sticks…" instead of "Do you struggle with…".
- **Curiosity gap:** "We didn't expect [result] from a [product]." / "Nadie te dice esto sobre [categoría]."
- **Social proof:** "40,000+ people switched to [product]. Here's why." / "4.8★ across 12,000 reseñas."
- **Specific number / speed of claim:** concrete beats vague ("2 minutes", "1 ingredient", "30-day"). ([Nick Theriot hook frameworks](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBVgPnCPXb4))
- **Us-vs-old-way:** "Most [category] do X. We do Y."
- **Question that isn't a personal attribute:** "What if [category] actually [benefit]?"

DTC context 2026: **statics often outperform video**, prioritize **creative/copy volume** ("Andromeda needs constant fresh creative"), and **long-form primary text can outperform short-form** for warm/considered products — but the front-loaded first line still rules. ([coreyhaines31](https://github.com/coreyhaines31/marketingskills/blob/main/skills/ads/SKILL.md))

## 5. Emoji & formatting norms

- **0–2 purposeful emojis** per primary text; use them as visual anchors (✔️ list, → CTA), never to replace words or clutter the "See more" line.
- **Short lines + line breaks** for scannability on mobile; avoid ALL-CAPS walls (reads as spam and can hurt policy review).
- Bullets (• or ✔) work for feature lists but keep the first ~125 chars prose so the hook reads naturally before "See more."
- Match the **landing page** message and tone (message match lifts conversion; mismatched clickbait tanks it). ([adsuploader](https://adsuploader.com/blog/meta-ad-copy-specs))

## 6. Compliance — Meta ad policy landmines

Meta uses **semantic intent detection** — it reads the *implied* meaning of copy, not just banned keywords. ([auditsocials — Meta ad policy updates](https://www.auditsocials.com/blog/meta-ad-policy-updates-2026-guide))

- **Personal attributes (the #1 DTC rejection):** never assert or imply you know the reader's **health, body/weight, mental health, finances, age, religion, political views, or relationship status.** The classic violators are second-person problem callouts: "Struggling with belly fat?", "Do you have high blood sugar?", "¿Sufres de ansiedad?". Enforcement now covers **indirect second-person framing** too. **Fix:** reframe to third person or product-first ("This routine helps with…", "La rutina que…"). ([Meta Transparency Center — Personal Attributes](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/objectionable-content/privacy-violations-personal-attributes/), [auditsocials](https://www.auditsocials.com/blog/meta-ad-policy-updates-2026-guide))
- **Before/after & implied transformations:** banned — including a product shown next to a fit/healthy person, or "See results in 7 days" over a transformation image. Treated as misleading even without the word "guarantee." ([auditsocials](https://www.auditsocials.com/blog/meta-ad-policy-updates-2026-guide))
- **Unrealistic / exaggerated claims:** no guaranteed results, no medical claims you can't substantiate.
- **Only real proof:** never fabricate review counts, ratings, press, or testimonials; use exactly what the brief supplies.

**Copy safety pass = rewrite any line that (a) implies knowledge of a personal attribute, (b) promises a specific/before-after transformation, or (c) cites proof you can't back.**

## 7. Quick QA checklist before delivering

1. Awareness level chosen and copy matches it.
2. ≥2 primary texts on **different** angles (pain-led + benefit-led/social-proof-led).
3. Each primary text's **first ~125 chars stand alone** as a full mini-ad.
4. One framework per block; one value prop + one proof + one CTA.
5. 2 headlines ≤40 chars (aim ≤27 for Feed); 1 description ≤30 chars.
6. 0–2 emojis; scannable line breaks.
7. Compliance pass done (no personal attributes, no before/after, real proof only).
8. Correct language/market/currency; display link present.
